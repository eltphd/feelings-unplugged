Educator Toolkit: Classroom Emotion Regulation

For Teachers, Counselors & Youth Workers

Introduction

This toolkit adapts Feelings Unplugged for educational settings. Use these resources to:

  • Integrate emotional regulation into existing curriculum
  • Support students experiencing emotional distress
  • Create trauma-informed classroom culture
  • Build SEL (Social-Emotional Learning) capacity

Quick-Start Guide: 5-Minute Daily Check-In

Use at the start of class to help students regulate before learning.

Format:

  • Mood Meter: Students plot emotion on 2-axis grid (energy level + pleasant/unpleasant)
  • Share-Out (Optional): "If you'd like to share your mood or what's affecting it, you can."
  • Grounding: 30-second breathing exercise
  • Transition: "Now we're ready to learn together."

Materials: Printable mood meter (see resources section)

Lesson Plan: Introduction to Emotion Regulation

45-Minute Classroom Activity

Grade Level: 7-12

Standards Alignment: CASEL SEL Framework (Self-Awareness, Self-Management)

Learning Objectives:

Students will be able to:

  • Identify and name complex emotions
  • Understand the function of emotions
  • Practice one emotion regulation strategy

Materials:

  • Emotion wheel handout
  • Journal prompts
  • Markers/colored pencils
  • Chart paper

Lesson Flow:

Opening (5 min):

"Today we're talking about emotions. Not 'good' or 'bad' emotions—all emotions are data. They're your brain's way of telling you something."

Activity 1: Emotion Vocabulary (10 min)

  • Display emotion wheel
  • Students identify 3 emotions they felt this week
  • Share: "What's an emotion you feel often but don't have a word for?"

Activity 2: Function of Emotions (10 min)

  • Anger: Signals boundary violation or injustice
  • Sadness: Signals loss or unmet need
  • Fear: Signals threat (real or perceived)
  • Joy: Signals alignment with values
  • Disgust: Signals something harmful

Activity 3: Regulation Practice (15 min)

Teach ONE strategy:

  • Box breathing (4 counts in, 4 hold, 4 out, 4 hold)
  • Grounding (5-4-3-2-1 senses)
  • Emotion journaling (3-minute free write)

Closing (5 min):

"Emotions aren't the problem. It's what we do with them that matters. This week, notice when you feel strong emotions and what you do to manage them."

Homework: Complete 3 journal prompts from Feelings Unplugged

Supporting Students in Crisis

Warning Signs

Watch for:

  • Sudden withdrawal or behavior changes
  • Declining grades
  • Comments about hopelessness ("I don't matter" / "What's the point?")
  • Giving away possessions
  • Self-harm (cuts, burns on arms/legs)
  • Excessive fatigue or hypervigilance

Immediate Response Protocol

If a student discloses suicidal thoughts:

  • Stay calm
  • Listen: "I'm glad you told me."
  • Don't promise secrecy: "I care about you, which means I need to connect you with help."
  • Walk them to counselor immediately (don't leave them alone)
  • Document: Time, what was said, action taken
  • Follow school protocol: Notify counselor, admin, parents
  • Provide resources: 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

If a student discloses abuse:

  • Believe them: "Thank you for trusting me."
  • Don't interrogate: Let them share at their pace
  • Mandatory reporting: You are legally required to report to CPS/police
  • Explain next steps: "I'm going to talk to [counselor/admin] because it's my job to keep you safe."
  • Follow school protocol

Resources:

  • Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline: 1-800-422-4453
  • RAINN Sexual Assault Hotline: 1-800-656-4673

Culturally Responsive Practices

For Black Students:

Recognize:

  • Racial trauma (witnessing police violence, microaggressions)
  • Code-switching fatigue
  • Stereotype threat in academic settings
  • "Strong Black woman/man" trope pressure

Do:

  • Create space to process racism without tone-policing
  • Validate anger as appropriate response to injustice
  • Use culturally affirming language
  • Highlight Black excellence and joy, not just trauma

Resources: BEAM (Black Emotional & Mental Health Collective), BlackLine: 1-800-604-5841

For LGBTQ+ Students:

Recognize:

  • Minority stress (discrimination, family rejection)
  • Higher rates of suicidality (especially trans youth)
  • Lack of representation in curriculum
  • Bathroom/locker room safety concerns

Do:

  • Use correct names/pronouns (even if parents deadname)
  • Display Pride flags, safe space stickers
  • Include LGBTQ+ topics in curriculum
  • Challenge homophobic/transphobic language immediately

Resources: Trevor Project: 1-866-488-7386, GLSEN (educator training)

For Immigrant/Multilingual Students:

Recognize:

  • Language barriers in expressing emotions
  • Pressure to translate for family
  • Balancing home culture vs. school culture
  • Immigration-related trauma

Do:

  • Provide emotion vocabulary in native language
  • Don't assume proficiency = emotional wellness
  • Honor cultural differences in emotional expression
  • Connect with culturally matched counselors

Printable Resources

1. Emotion Wheel (Full Color)

  • Primary emotions (center): Happy, Sad, Angry, Afraid, Surprised, Disgusted
  • Secondary emotions (middle ring): 18 more specific emotions
  • Tertiary emotions (outer ring): 72 nuanced emotions

Use for: Expanding emotional vocabulary

2. Coping Strategies Menu

Physical: Go for a walk, Dance to music, Take a shower, Progressive muscle relaxation

Creative: Draw/paint, Write poetry, Play instrument, Craft/DIY project

Social: Text a friend, Hug someone safe, Join a club, Pet an animal

Cognitive: Journal, Meditate, Reframe thoughts, Make a gratitude list

Sensory: Light a candle, Listen to music, Hold ice cube, Smell essential oils

3. Grounding Technique Cards

5-4-3-2-1 Method:

  • 5 things you can see
  • 4 things you can touch
  • 3 things you can hear
  • 2 things you can smell
  • 1 thing you can taste

Box Breathing:

  • Breathe in for 4 counts
  • Hold for 4 counts
  • Breathe out for 4 counts
  • Hold for 4 counts
  • Repeat 4 times

Adapted Journal Prompts for Classroom

  • Monday Morning: "What energy are you bringing into this week?"
  • Midweek Check-In: "What emotion has shown up most this week? What's it trying to tell you?"
  • Friday Reflection: "What are you proud of this week? (Even small things count)"
  • Identity Exploration: "Which Academy Archetype resonates with you? (Catalyst, Luminary, Guardian, Maverick, Weaver) Why?"
  • Shadow Work (Advanced, Gr 10-12): "What part of yourself do you hide at school? What would it feel like to let it show?"

Educator Self-Care

You're holding a lot.

Compassion fatigue is real. Symptoms:

  • Emotional exhaustion
  • Cynicism
  • Reduced empathy
  • Physical symptoms (headaches, insomnia)

Boundaries you can set:

  • Office hours (don't be available 24/7)
  • Refer to counselor when appropriate
  • "I care, but I can't counsel" (you're a teacher, not a therapist)

Resources for you:

  • Teacher therapy directories
  • Educator wellness apps (Calm for Educators - free)
  • Peer support groups

Remember: You can't save everyone. You can show up consistently and care deeply. That's enough.
