A 120-Page Journey into Depth Work & Shadow Integration

What Makes This Different?

This isn't another gratitude journal with surface-level prompts. Feelings Unplugged is designed for teens who are ready to explore the complexity of their inner world—the parts they show the world and the parts they hide, even from themselves.

Combining Jungian psychology, neuroscience, and culturally responsive frameworks, this journal guides you through transformative self-discovery without judgment or prescription.

Core Premise: Your feelings aren't problems to solve. They're data to decode, shadows to integrate, and stories waiting to be witnessed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need therapy experience to use this journal?

No. While informed by therapeutic frameworks, this journal is designed for independent use. However, it can complement therapy beautifully. If you're working with a therapist, consider sharing your journal entries during sessions.

What if the prompts feel too intense?

Skip them. Come back later. Go at your pace. There's a "regulation toolkit" section specifically for moments when emotional intensity feels overwhelming.

Is this culturally appropriate for all teens?

The journal includes frameworks for cultural identity exploration and explicitly honors diverse experiences. However, it's written from a Western psychology lens integrated with cultural humility. Use what resonates; adapt what doesn't.

Can parents/teachers read this with teens?

The journal is designed for teen privacy and autonomy. We recommend teens decide what (if anything) they want to share. For guided family/classroom work, see our Parent Guide and Educator Toolkit.
